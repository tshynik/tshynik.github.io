The Companion

by E.E. Ottoman

no star rating

14 July 2023

Not enough plot for me, goes way too fast into relationships. Also I get making up the history you wish you had, but IMHO in that case you have to either commit to the bit that there is no transphobia or seriously think through the alternate history and its logic --- making the milieu vaguely transphobic only when useful to get everyone in the same room is lazy. (This is the sci fi nerd in me, sorry not sorry!) I would buy this as taking place outside Berlin sometime 1900-1925ish where there was a trans community that were starting to understand themselves as trans in closer to the modern sense, but as far as I'm aware this conception of gender identity doesn't really make sense in New York pre-Christine Jorgensen. ETA: Apparently Harry Benjamin did set up a practice in NYC in 1948, but a) a little too soon for this story, and b) his treatment was all aimed towards going stealth.
