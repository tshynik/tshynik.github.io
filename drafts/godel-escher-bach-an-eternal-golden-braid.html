Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid

by Douglas R. Hofstadter

no star rating

(6 Oct 2010) I haven't thought about this for a while, even though it's sitting on my bookshelf.

I did math research in Summer 2009 at Univ. of Indiana at Bloomington (where the author teaches!), and I lived with a dozen college-age math nerds. Two of them were also reading it or had read parts of it. We eventually decided that this book is written for people who don't know math. Hofstadter spends ages and ages going the long way around to explain relatively simple concepts (for someone with the math background, anyway). Eventually, he would get to the point and we'd say "OH! He was talking about that! But I already knew that, and this explanation is much more vague and confusing."

Granted, I haven't read the whole thing and I probably never will. I find the writing incredibly obnoxious: I know some people love the Socratic dialogues, but I can't stand them. I have better ways to spend my time---and if I want to understand the math, I'll read it in the original, thanks all the same.

(18 July 2008) I got a hankering to work on it today, but it's locked away in Howell for the summer. Boo.

(21 May 2008) I'm working through this, but don't hold your breath. I don't expect to finish anytime soon.
