Dead Witch Walking (The Hollows, #1)

by Kim Harrison

It's not fair of me to rate this book when I didn't get more than 50 pages into it, but I Did Not Like. The writing is choppy and awkward and embarrassing to read. I guess I can (eventually, probably) get behind a character who makes huge, complicated, and probably dangerous decisions on the slightest of whims, but it doesn't make a compelling plot, it just makes me wonder if any of the rest of the book is going to make sense, either.

Anyway, I stuck it out until the part where the main character, in first-person, gives a giant infodump about how HISTORY TURNED OUT DIFFERENT BECAUSE WE DIDN'T MAKE IT TO THE MOON AND DID BIOTECH INSTEAD. I have a sneaking suspicion that most advancements in bioengineering were only possible because of the improved computers (not to mention mechanical and materials engineering) that were byproducts of the space race, but I admit I'm not an expert. Maybe it's possible. Maybe it's even possible that a virus can "use a weak spot in a tomato gene" to piggyback on the plant, although I don't have the foggiest idea what "weak spot" is supposed to mean in this context. But I can't fucking forgive a character who seems to know all about how some alternate history would have turned out---not finding another way to phrase that is sheer cussed laziness.

Not even the promise of queer ladies is enough to make me press on---although all the reviewers here who are vocally disappointed in the lack of hunky male love interests had me seriously considering it.
