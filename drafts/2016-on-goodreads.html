I read more than I expected to in 2016. Once I managed to hit 50 a few years ago, to prove that I could, I've been more relaxed about it. This year's high count is influenced by short stories, but I read plenty of long stuff too---the average number of pages per book in 2016 was 237, which is respectable even if it's a little below my average book size of 308 pages for the previous 10 years, and is better than 2015 (217 pages long on average).

I set myself a goal this year of reading at least half books by authors of color and at least half by women. Did I make it? Could have done better. I partly blame the US election; I dove into distracting comfort reads for a month or so, rather than the more challenging stuff I had planned.

Total: 68 books, including 15 short stories (all of which are free online!) and 13 graphic novels.

At least one author who's a woman: 39 (57.4%) At least one author of color: 19 (27.9%) At least one LGBT author: 30 (44.1%) Straight white men only: 12 (17.6%), so not!SWM = 82.4% of what I read

New year, new try! My primary goal is to read more than half authors of color this year.
